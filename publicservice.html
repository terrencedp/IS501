Public Service & Ethics

My Projected Career Path

I hope to work for the Philippine government doing research and policy analysis. I would like to focus on promoting good governance, ethical leadership, and accountability in government. I chose Political Science as an undergraduate degree because I want to learn the best practices in the American government and implement them in my home country. As a Master of Public Administration student, I am focusing on gaining leadership and practical skills that I will need to serve my fellow Filipinos.


Experience in the Field

I had an opportunity to do an internship at the Governor's Office in Isabela, one of the largest provincial governments in the Philippines. I have worked closely with the Provincial Administrator, who supervises the day-to-day operation of the provincial government. I performed administrative tasks to support the work of eight key department heads. This enables me to work with various individuals and organizations to organized programs and policies to serve the people of Isabela. My experience working for a state-level government in the Philippines has led me to realize the importance of research and analytical skills to formulate policies and programs.

Provincial Capitol in Isabela, Philippines



Anticipated Contribution to the Field

I am currently leading a team researching ethical dilemmas in the public sector. As a research associate for BYU Public Service Lab, I am responsible for conducting literature searches, data management, recruiting participants, obtaining consent, maintaining files, scheduling and conducting interviews, maintaining data collection files, assisting with data analysis, and generating correspondence and reports. We hope to develop a field guidebook that will help current and future public administrators provide guidance and resources when faced with ethical dilemmas in decision-making. I hope to contribute to responsible, ethical, and clean government practices and decision-making.

For more information please visit BYU Public Service Lab.
